Young physio business expanding after one year

TIFFANY LEWIS Telegraph-Journal

    Share this article
  • Facebook
  • Twitter
  • Email

July 18, 2017

SAINT JOHN • One year ago, Frances Roderick took a leap of faith. Today, the physiotherapist who started Archstone Physiotherapy and Wellness Centre is expanding her practice and moving to a new location in Rothesay.

“Initially I had just planned to go out on my own and start this,” she said. “I wasn’t quite sure where it was going to go.”

Roderick brought on a massage therapist, and the two work out of an office on Drury Cove Road. Roderick focuses on women’s health. 

“[I] was focusing on areas around the pelvis, the pelvic floor and the lower abdominal area,” she said. “Because initially I was doing a lot of high performance running assessments and a lot of the women were coming in and saying, ‘I’ve had to stop running because I’m leaking when I run.' ... So I realized I need to start paying more attention to this.”

Roderick said the need to expand came from an increase in clients coming in to get treated for issues many don't like to talk about. The physiotherapist said her business is thriving just through word of mouth.

“The word is getting out and it’s awesome because we’re getting really busy,” she said. “People are actually recognizing that, ‘No, I don’t have to put up with this. I can come and get this treated and I can have my life back.’ And that’s the message people give to me. Even then we’ve branched out because not just women have pelvic floors, men have pelvic floors, too.”

Roderick said she will be adding more staff to the team.

“We actually have a pediatric physiotherapist [and] she’s got a tremendous amount of experience,” Roderick said. “She actually worked here in Saint John for a period of time and then moved to Australia for the last 15 years and did a lot of work in physio down there.”

Roderick will be moving her practice to the Rothesay town hall on July 31. She said she has been very pleased with Saint John’s response to the business.

“When you go out on your own, you’re never quite sure what to expect. I have to say there were moments when we made the decision to open this clinic where we [said], ‘Oh wow, what did [we] do?’ But I did know that the market was there, and it [was] just [the question of], were we going to be able to tap into it?”

Roderick said she will be bringing on a certified hand specialist to the team as well.

“We’ll be able to offer quite a bit more services as we expand,” Roderick said.
